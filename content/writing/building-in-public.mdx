There's a growing movement of makers who share their progress openly. Instead of polishing things behind closed doors and doing a big reveal, they post updates, share metrics, and talk about what they're learning.

## Why it works

Building in public does a few things:

1. **Accountability** — When you tell people what you're working on, you feel more motivated to follow through.
2. **Feedback loops** — Early feedback helps you course-correct before you've invested too much time.
3. **Community** — People rally around transparency. It's refreshing in a world of curated perfection.

## The uncomfortable parts

Not everything is glamorous. You'll share bugs, bad decisions, and slow weeks. That's the point — it normalizes the messy reality of building things.

<Callout type="tip" title="Start Small">
You don't have to share everything. Start with a weekly update or a monthly reflection. Find the cadence that works for you.
</Callout>

## How I approach it

I use this site as my primary outlet. Longer essays go here, shorter thoughts go on social media. The key is consistency over volume.

> The best time to start writing was years ago. The second best time is now.

Writing clarifies thinking. Building in public is just thinking out loud.
